# 5W1H Methodology

Systematic questioning approach using Who, What, When, Where, Why, and How to drive comprehensive requirements elicitation.

## Who — Stakeholders and Users

Identify everyone involved in or affected by the system.

- Who are the primary users of the system?
- Who are the secondary stakeholders?
- Who will be responsible for system maintenance?
- Who has decision-making authority?

## What — System Functionality

Define what the system must do and the boundaries it operates within.

- What functions should the system perform?
- What data will the system process?
- What outputs should the system produce?
- What are the system boundaries?

## When — Timing and Scheduling

Determine time-related constraints and operational windows.

- When should the system be available?
- When do specific functions need to execute?
- When are peak usage periods?
- When should data be updated?

## Where — Deployment and Environment

Identify where the system lives and how users reach it.

- Where will the system be deployed?
- Where will users access the system?
- Where is data stored and processed?
- Where are the integration points with external systems?

## Why — Business Rationale

Surface the underlying objectives that justify each requirement.

- Why is this system needed?
- Why are specific features required?
- Why are certain constraints imposed?
- Why is this the preferred solution approach?

## How — Implementation Approach

Explore interaction models and integration mechanics.

- How will users interact with the system?
- How will the system integrate with existing systems?
- How will data be processed and stored?
- How will security be implemented?

## Application Guidelines

### Preparation

- Review existing documentation before sessions
- Identify and schedule key stakeholders
- Prepare question templates tailored to the domain
- Plan the interview structure and time allocation

### Execution

- Start with high-level questions to establish context
- Drill down into specifics based on responses
- Allow natural follow-up questions to surface hidden needs
- Document responses systematically during the session

### Follow-up

- Validate your understanding with stakeholders after each session
- Identify gaps and inconsistencies across sessions
- Prioritize areas requiring additional investigation
- Update requirements documentation with findings

## Benefits

- Comprehensive coverage of all requirements dimensions
- Systematic and repeatable structure that is easy to learn
- Helps surface missing or implicit requirements
- Facilitates clear communication between analysts and stakeholders
